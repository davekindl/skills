# Reference: Hungarian Civil Code (Ptk.) — Key Sections

Background reference for "why does the contract say this?" questions. Not loaded during generation. Not legal advice.

The governing statute is **Act V of 2013 on the Civil Code** (2013. évi V. törvény a Polgári Törvénykönyvről, "Ptk."). Section numbers use the Ptk. book:chapter format (e.g. 6:58 = Book Six, §58).

## Contracts — General (Book Six)
- **6:58–6:59** — Definition and freedom of contract. Parties may freely determine content within the law.
- **6:62** — Duty to cooperate and inform during contracting; pre-contractual good faith.
- **6:63** — When a contract is concluded; agreement on essential terms.
- **6:70** — Form requirements. Most consultancy contracts are valid without a specific form, but written form is strongly advisable for evidence.
- **6:78** — Incorporation of standard contract terms (ÁSZF). The other party must have had a genuine opportunity to know the terms; unusual terms require specific highlighting.
- **6:86** — Interpretation of contracts (according to common meaning and the parties' presumed intent).

## Standard Terms & Consumer Protection
- **6:77–6:81** — General terms and conditions: definition, incorporation, conflicting terms, unfair terms.
- **6:102–6:103** — Unfair terms in consumer contracts are void; the list of presumptively unfair terms.
- Note: B2B vs B2C matters. A consultancy selling to businesses has more freedom than one selling to consumers (where the ÁSZF / Tos faces stricter unfairness control).

## Performance, Breach & Liability
- **6:130** — Time and place of performance; monetary performance.
- **6:137–6:144** — Breach of contract: definition and consequences.
- **6:142** — Liability for damages caused by breach; exemption only if the breach was caused by an unforeseeable circumstance beyond control (the basis for force-majeure clauses).
- **6:152** — Limitation/exclusion of liability is **void** for damage caused intentionally or to life, bodily integrity, or health. This is why liability-cap clauses always carve out gross negligence and wilful misconduct.
- **6:186–6:189** — Penalty (kötbér): may be stipulated for non- or defective performance.

## Mandate / Services (relevant to consultancy)
- **6:272–6:280** — Mandate contract (megbízási szerződés): the typical legal form for consulting (best-efforts obligation), as opposed to a works contract (vállalkozási szerződés, 6:238+) which owes a defined result.
- A consultancy "advice" engagement is usually a mandate (obligation of conduct); a "build me X deliverable" engagement may be a works contract (obligation of result). This distinction affects warranty and acceptance.

## Limitation Period
- **6:22** — General limitation period is **5 years** unless otherwise provided.

## Why this matters for the toolkit
- Liability caps must carve out intent and harm to life/health (6:152) or they are partly void.
- Choosing mandate vs works-contract framing changes what the consultant owes (effort vs result).
- ÁSZF must be genuinely made available and unusual terms highlighted (6:78) or they don't bind.

> Section numbers are provided for orientation only and may have been amended. Verify against the consolidated text on njt.hu and confirm with a Hungarian lawyer.
