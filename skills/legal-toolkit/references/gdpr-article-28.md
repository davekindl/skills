# Reference: GDPR Article 28 — Processor Obligations

Background for the DPA template. Not loaded during generation. Not legal advice. Source: Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

## What Article 28 requires
When a controller uses a processor, the processing must be governed by a **contract** (or other legal act) that is binding on the processor and sets out the matters below. This is why a DPA is mandatory whenever you process a client's personal data through AI tools on the client's behalf.

## Art.28(1) — Sufficient guarantees
The controller may only use processors providing **sufficient guarantees** to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures.

## Art.28(2)–(4) — Sub-processors
- The processor shall not engage another processor **without prior authorisation** (specific or general) of the controller.
- With general authorisation, the processor must **inform the controller of intended changes** and give it the chance to **object**.
- The same data-protection obligations must be **flowed down** to sub-processors by contract.
(This is the legal root of the sub-processor list + change-notification clause.)

## Art.28(3) — Mandatory contract content
The DPA must stipulate that the processor:
1. **(a)** processes only on **documented instructions** from the controller, including on international transfers;
2. **(b)** ensures persons authorised to process are under a **confidentiality** obligation;
3. **(c)** takes all **security** measures required by **Article 32**;
4. **(d)** respects the conditions for engaging **sub-processors** (paras 2 and 4);
5. **(e)** assists the controller in responding to **data subject rights** requests;
6. **(f)** assists the controller with **security, breach notification, DPIA and prior consultation** (Arts 32–36);
7. **(g)** **deletes or returns** all personal data at the end of provision of services, and deletes existing copies (unless storage is required by law);
8. **(h)** makes available **information to demonstrate compliance** and allows for and contributes to **audits/inspections**.

Plus the contract must set out: the **subject matter, duration, nature and purpose** of processing, the **type of personal data** and **categories of data subjects**, and the **obligations and rights of the controller**.

## Art.28(3) second subparagraph — Whistleblow duty
The processor must **inform the controller** if an instruction infringes data-protection law.
