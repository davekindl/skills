# Reference: Lawyer Review Guide

How to turn a machine-generated draft from this toolkit into something safe to sign — and what to ask a Hungarian/EU lawyer. Not legal advice itself.

## When you MUST involve a lawyer (don't ship on the draft alone)
- First version of any template you'll reuse many times (consulting agreement, NDA, DPA, ÁSZF) — get it reviewed once, then reuse.
- Anything with liability above ~€10,000 exposure or a fixed-term exclusivity.
- Any DPA where client data is sensitive (health, financial, children).
- Anything touching **your employment non-compete**, if you have one — scope, client overlap, TEÁOR/industry-code overlap, naming.
- Cross-border contracts (non-Hungarian counterparty, non-Hungarian governing law).
- Consumer-facing terms (B2C) — unfair-terms control is strict.

## What a standard review costs
Roughly **€200–500** for a standard contract review in Hungary; a reusable template package costs more but amortises across every future client. A DPA or ÁSZF review typically sits at the higher end.

## How to read the [REVIEW] markers
Every draft from this toolkit flags clauses with `[REVIEW]`. These are the spots where a generic template most often goes wrong for a specific deal. Treat each `[REVIEW]` as a question to put to your lawyer, not as a finished clause.

## The "what to ask your lawyer" checklist
1. **Governing law & forum** — Is Hungarian law + the named court right for this counterparty? Should we arbitrate instead?
2. **Liability cap** — Is the multiplier (1x/2x fees) enforceable here, and are the carve-outs (intent, gross negligence, life/health per Ptk. 6:152) correctly stated?
3. **Mandate vs works contract** — Does this engagement owe *effort* (megbízás) or a *result* (vállalkozás)? Does the contract match?
4. **IP** — Does the client own deliverables while I keep my methodologies/tools? Is the assignment language effective under Hungarian copyright law?
5. **GDPR roles** — Am I controller, processor, or joint controller for this data? Is the DPA's Article 28 content complete?
6. **Sub-processors & transfers** — Are my AI/cloud sub-processors disclosed, and is the EU-US transfer basis valid today?
7. **AI Act** — Are my transparency/disclosure obligations (Art.50) correctly reflected for the AI I'm actually using?
8. **Termination & payment-on-termination** — Can either side exit cleanly, and do I get paid for work done?
9. **Non-compete safety** — Does anything here conflict with my employment non-compete (scope, clients, TEÁOR codes)?
10. **Tax/VAT framing** — Is the invoicing/VAT treatment (e.g. EU B2B reverse-charge) stated correctly? (Confirm with the accountant, not only the lawyer.)

## Division of labour
- **Lawyer (ügyvéd):** contract enforceability, liability, IP, GDPR/AI Act, non-compete.
- **Accountant (könyvelő):** VAT treatment, invoicing, tax filings, Kft. annual deadlines.
- **You:** the commercial terms (price, scope, timeline) — the toolkit just structures them.

## Finding the right lawyer
Look for a Hungarian lawyer with **IT / data-protection / commercial contract** experience (preferably some AI/GDPR exposure). A local practice near your registered seat, or a Budapest firm, both work fine.

> This guide is orientation, not advice. Costs and procedures change; confirm current rates and scope with the specific lawyer you engage.
