# Persuasion Hooks for Business Evolution Teasers

Applied from Cialdini's 7 principles. Only 4 are used in cold outreach teasers.

## The 4 Active Principles

### 1. Authority (ALWAYS use)
Position as methodology, not opinion.

**Patterns:**
- "47-point assessment across [N] dimensions"
- "Using the same digital maturity framework applied by [known consultancy]"
- "Based on [research source] benchmarks"
- Numbers > adjectives: "3.2x slower" beats "much slower"

**Anti-pattern:** Don't name-drop without substance. "As McKinsey would say" without actual McKinsey data is empty authority.

### 2. Social Proof (ALWAYS use when sentiment data exists)
Quote their OWN users. This is the most powerful hook because it's unanswerable.

**Patterns:**
- "On [Reddit/Trustpilot/Google Reviews], your users say: '[verbatim quote]'"
- "Users comparing you to [competitor]: '[comparison quote]'"
- "[N] reviews mention [specific pain point] in the last 6 months"
- "Your app has [X] stars vs [competitor]'s [Y] stars on [store]"

**Anti-pattern:** Don't cherry-pick unfairly. Use representative sentiment, not the single worst review. Include their strengths too (builds credibility).

### 3. Scarcity (use when competitive movement is recent)
Time-sensitive competitive gap.

**Patterns:**
- "[Competitor] launched [feature] [specific date]. First-mover window closing."
- "Every month without [gap], an estimated €[X] in [metric] is unrealized"
- "GEO optimization: 50% of AI-cited content is less than 13 weeks old. Your last content update was [date]."

**Anti-pattern:** Don't fabricate urgency. If there's no real time pressure, don't invent it. Use naturally occurring urgency only.

### 4. Commitment (use in the CTA)
Small initial ask that leads to larger engagement.

**Patterns:**
- "20-minute call -- no pitch, just the data"
- The 3-page PDF itself is a commitment device: 2 minutes of reading investment
- "If [specific finding #1] and [specific finding #2] resonate..." -- makes them self-select

**Anti-pattern:** Don't ask for a big commitment upfront. "Schedule a 2-hour strategy session" in a cold email = delete.

## The 3 Avoided Principles (and why)

| Principle | Why NOT in Cold Outreach |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Liking** | Creates sycophancy. "I love what you've built!" in a cold email about their flaws is dissonant. |
| **Reciprocity** | The free teaser IS the reciprocity, but don't make it explicit. "I did this for you, so..." feels transactional. |
| **Unity** | No shared identity exists yet. "As fellow innovators..." to a stranger is cringe. |

## Hook Construction Formula

```
SUBJECT: [Social Proof or Scarcity -- the most specific finding]

LINE 1: [Authority] We [audited/analyzed/benchmarked] [target] against
        [Tier 3 competitor] across [N] dimensions.

LINE 2-3: [Social Proof] Your users [quote or comparison data].
          [Competitor] addresses this with [specific feature/approach].

LINE 4-5: [Scarcity] [Competitive movement or market timing].
          Estimated impact: €[conservative figure] annually.

LINE 6-7: [Commitment] 3-page preview attached. If [finding 1] and
          [finding 2] resonate, 20 minutes for the full walkthrough.
```

## Hungarian Adaptation

For Hungarian targets, ALWAYS route through hungarian-content-gate before sending.

Additional HU-specific persuasion adjustments:
- **Authority:** Hungarians respect institutional credibility. Reference EU frameworks, ISO standards, or Magyar Kereskedelmi Kamara patterns.
- **Social Proof:** Hungarian forums (Prohardver, HWsw.hu fórum, gyakorikerdesek.hu) are higher-value sources than international ones for HU targets.
- **Register:** Always Ön (formal B2B). Never te.
- **Directness:** Hungarian business culture values directness more than Anglo cultures. The "no pitch, just data" framing resonates strongly.
